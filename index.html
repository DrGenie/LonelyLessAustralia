LonelyLessAustralia Decision Aid Tool

Loneliness affects over five million Australians, especially older adults, resulting in serious health and economic burdens. This decision aid tool is based on a discrete choice experiment (DCE) that identified older adults’ preferences for programmes aimed at reducing loneliness in Australia. By incorporating these DCE estimates, the tool predicts uptake probabilities for various programme packages, offering personalised services based on the factors older adults prioritise when seeking support.

The loneliness categories used in this study were derived from the De Jong Gierveld Loneliness Scale (11-item version), which measures emotional and social loneliness. Summing these yields a total loneliness score, subsequently classified into three levels—Not Lonely, Moderately Lonely, and Severely Lonely—for more nuanced comparisons across different severity categories.

How to Use This Tool

1. Read about Attributes to understand each dimension of the programme.

2. In Inputs, select a state (if any), cost-of-living, cost per session, accessibility, frequency, method, duration, and type of support.

3. Click “Calculate & View Results” to open a detailed results window with advanced cost-benefit charts, predicted uptake probabilities, optional PDF exports, etc.

4. Save your scenario under the Scenarios tab to compare multiple configurations side by side.

5. The WTP Results tab displays bar plots summarising how much individuals would pay (main population and by loneliness category).

6. The Program Uptake Probability tab offers bar charts for predicted uptake probability in the main model and each loneliness severity group.

7. In Costs & Benefits, explore cost assumptions, including a \$5,000 benefit for each 1% increase in programme uptake.

Programme Attributes

Cost: Out-of-pocket cost per session for participants.

Accessibility: Local area or wider community travel distance, factoring mobility constraints.

Frequency of Interaction: Daily (reference), weekly, monthly, etc.

Method of Interaction: In-person, virtual-only, or hybrid approach.

Duration of Each Interaction: Session lengths (30 minutes, 2 hours, 4 hours, etc.).

Type of Support Programme: Peer support, community engagement, counselling, VR, etc.

Cost-of-living multipliers may apply based on the state selected. Next, visit Inputs to set your scenario.

Cost Components & Benefits

We assume a cost-of-living multiplier (per state) and a benefit of A\$5,000 for each 1% improvement in programme uptake. Cost elements can include:

  • Staff/Personnel
  • Facilities & Equipment
  • Marketing & Outreach
  • Training & Certification
  • Miscellaneous Operational Expenses

These represent a realistic starting point for cost and benefit assumptions within the tool.
