LonelyLessAustralia Decision Aid Tool

Introduction

Loneliness affects over five million Australians, particularly older adults, creating significant health and economic burdens. This online decision aid tool is developed from a discrete choice experiment that identified older adults’ preferences for various support programmes designed to reduce loneliness in Australia. Using the experiment’s insights and estimates, this tool predicts the probability of uptake for different programme packages, enabling personalised services based on the key factors older adults value when seeking social connection.

How to Use This Tool

Welcome to LonelyLessAustralia! This tool helps you assess the likelihood of programme uptake aimed at reducing loneliness among older adults in Australia. Follow these steps:

  1. Select your preferences from the drop-down menus.
  2. Click “Calculate Probability” to see your results.
  3. Review your predicted probability, selected programme package, and cost-benefit analysis.
  4. Download your selected programme package and Uptake Probability chart for future reference.
  5. Use the buttons below to view detailed analyses by loneliness category (Not Lonely, Moderately Lonely, Severely Lonely) and WTP (AUD).

These support programmes are vital for fostering community engagement and improving well-being among older adults.
